Course Title:
Professional Issues in Information Systems

Course Code:
IPC 3

Learning Outcome 1 (LO1):
Critique the foundational ethical theories and professional codes of conduct that govern the Information Systems industry.

Topics under LO1:
1. Ethical Frameworks in Computing
   - Utilitarianism vs. Deontology in Tech
   - Virtue Ethics for Developers
   - Slippery Slope of Minor Ethical Lapses

2. Professional Codes of Conduct
   - ACM Code of Ethics
   - IEEE Code of Conduct
   - Professional Certification and Accountability

3. Ethics in IS Practice
   - Ethical Decision-Making in IS
   - Professional Responsibility
   - Trust and Integrity in Technology


Learning Outcome 2 (LO2):
Assess the legal frameworks surrounding intellectual property, data privacy, and cybercrime within local and international contexts.

Topics under LO2:
1. Intellectual Property in the Digital Age
   - Software Patents vs. Copyright
   - Open Source Licensing (GPL, MIT)
   - Trade Secrets and NDAs

2. Data Privacy and Protection Laws
   - GDPR, CCPA, and Local Privacy Acts
   - Principles of Data Minimization
   - Right to be Forgotten

3. Cybercrime and Legal Responsibility
   - Computer Fraud and Abuse Acts
   - Liability for Security Breaches
   - Digital Forensics and Chain of Custody


Learning Outcome 3 (LO3):
Evaluate the societal impacts of emerging technologies, including issues of digital equity, diversity, and algorithmic bias.

Topics under LO3:
1. The Digital Divide and Social Equity
   - Global Access to Technology
   - Assistive Technologies for Disability
   - Impact of Automation on Labor

2. Algorithmic Bias and AI Ethics
   - Bias in Machine Learning Training Data
   - Black Box Problem
   - Ethical Implications of Autonomous Systems

3. Environmental Sustainability in IS
   - E-waste Management
   - Energy Consumption of Data Centers
   - Sustainable Procurement Policies


Learning Outcome 4 (LO4):
Synthesize professional strategies to resolve complex workplace dilemmas while maintaining organizational policy and public trust.

Topics under LO4:
1. Whistleblowing and Workplace Integrity
   - Internal vs. External Reporting
   - Legal Protections for Whistleblowers
   - Loyalty to Employer vs. Public Safety

2. Conflict of Interest and Professional Boundaries
   - Gifts and Bribery in Procurement
   - Moonlighting and IP Ownership
   - Relationships with Clients and Vendors

3. Crisis Management and Communication
   - Ethical Communication During System Failure
   - Transparency with Stakeholders
   - Rebuilding Trust After a Professional Lapse
